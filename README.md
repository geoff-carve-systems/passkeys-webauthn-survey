# Passkeys in the Wild

[Passkeys in the Wild](https://snowfroc.com/schedule) was presented by Geoff Robinson at OWASP's SnowFROC Security Conference on April 17, 2026.

The PowerPoint slide deck is available [here](https://github.com/ivision-research/presentations/tree/master/2026/snowfroc).

The data files and scripts associated with this research are maintained [here](https://github.com/geoff-carve-systems/passkeys-webauthn-survey).

A recording of the talk is forthcoming on YouTube.

> WebAuthn and Passkeys - elements of the FIDO2 passwordless authentication scheme -- continue to become more commonplace now that they are supported by all of the major browsers. Passkeys promise to be "more secure" by avoiding the pitfalls of passwords, but the huge variety of WebAuthn server implementations are relatively untested. And we know that even if the protocol is bulletproof, a given deployment can be anything but. As pen testers, what tricks can we still use to spoof authentication or elevate privileges? In this talk, Geoff will review his efforts to systematically document Passkey deployments in the wild and fingerprint their underlying implementations. This is part of a larger research project to identify vulnerabilities and patterns of weaknesses in deployed WebAuthn and Passkey based authentication systems.
